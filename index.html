Introduction to BCI

Forget keyboards, forget touchscreens – think it, do it! Introducing BCIs.

The idea of having the potential or ability to control one's surroundings with thoughts had only being existed in science fiction. But with the recent advancements in the Artificial Intelligence had now made this futuristic thinking really possible and workable!!! Invention of Brain Computer Interface have opened the doors of a future where our brain’s electrical signals will become the channels of communication with computers overcome disability and tap into the limitless potential of mind. A BCI is a direct communication pathway between the brain’s electrical activity and an external device, most commonly a computer or robotic limb, BCI analyzes brain signals, and translates them into commands that are relayed to an output device to carry out a desired action. The Study of Brain Computer Interface (BCI) comes under the domain of neuroscience.

Applications of BCI

BCIs: Giving a voice to the silent, a second chance to walk, and opening doors to a future we can only imagine.

1.Restoring Mobility and Communication for Person with Disabilities:
For people with paralysis: BCIs can control prosthetic limbs, wheelchairs, or robotic exoskeletons, enabling them to regain movement and independence.
2. Advancing Education and Learning:
Brain-controlled training simulations: BCIs can create immersive training environments for pilots, surgeons, or other professionals, enhancing skill development and performance.
3. Redefining Human-Computer Interaction and Entertainment:
Hands-free control of devices: BCIs can enable intuitive control of computers, smartphones, and other smart home devices through thought alone.

How it Works

BCIs: Decoding the symphony of your brain, one neuron at a time.

A Brain Computer Interface works by interpreting the electrical signals from the brain and amplifying with use of electroencephalography sensors called EEG signals. BCI uses the technique called as MIND which is an acronym of Measure, Interpret, Encode, and Deploy. An EEG uses small metallic electrodes to capture the electrical signals. After the reading of the electrical signals, these signals are transformed into recognizable patterns by feature extractions. These patterns become input of various Machine Learning Algorithms like GAN, VAE(variational autoencoders), CNN etc.

Types of BCIs

Electrodes, lasers, and thought waves, oh my! Exploring the diverse realm of BCI types

Forget batons and podiums, folks! We're entering the concert hall of the mind, where the melody is your thoughts and the conductor is… your brain-computer interface! But hold on, there's not just one maestro in town. Let's explore the three main ways to orchestrate your brainwaves:

Invasive BCI:

Invasive BCIs are implanted directly into the grey matter of the brain during neurosurgery, this chips implanted against the brain that have hundreds of pins piercing the cerebral cortex.

Partially Invasive BCI:

In this small part of device is implanted inside the skull and other outside the brain rather than within the grey matter. The signal strength of these are weaker than the Invasive BCI

Non-Invasive BCI:

Non-invasive BCI are communication systems that rely on direct, noninvasive measures of brain activity. The signal strength of these are weaker than the other two forms.
